Software Design Life Cycles

Main Plan Implement Test & Deploy

Every product in market has some kind of life cycle that
measures its existence from ideation to the time when
it is finally pulled off the shelves for good. Software
is no different.

The Software Development Life Cycle (or SDLC) is a
system or process that governs how software developers
design, build, test, and deploy their products.

There are many different SDLCs, and different
insitutions may give you different definitions and
varying numbers of steps on how to proceed while
developing software.

With that being said, there is an international SDLC
Standard. It is called ISO/IEC 12207, and it wants to
be the one true standard for all Software Development in
in the universe.

I am just going to stick to four basic steps, ones that
I was made aware of while testing software for a living.

Plan, Implement, Test, and Deploy.
