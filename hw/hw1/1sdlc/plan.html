The Planning stage is essential for Software Development. This stage should, in a
perfect world, involve many meetings and conversations between all the stake
holders involved. The purpose of this initial effort to make sure everyone
is on the same page in regards to requirements, cost and risks. The stakeholders
could involve representatives from every department in the company, so clear
communication is key. As software developers, it is in our best interests to ask
as many questions as possible.

Imagine for a second that you run a team of programmers, and someone from marketing
scribbles some notes on a piece of paper and tells you he needs the changes
implemented immediately. Is this change offical? Does he have approval to make this
request? Once you finish the changes, is he going to decide he doesn't like it,
then pull your team off other projects, again, to make more frivolous changes until
he is finally satisfied?

A good way to avoid all that is to have an in depth discussion with the person,
pose many questions, and get him to think more critically about what the changes
he truly requires. Also, it helps ensure our understanding of those requirements.
