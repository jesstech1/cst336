The first OS, GMOS, was created by General Motors to run IBM's 701 Machine back in the 50s.
Originally OSs were called single-stream batch processing systems. Data submission
was limited to single group submissions. The computers of the 50s, or mainframes, were
large enough in scale to fill a large room, and required professional operators to run them.
Access was limited to government agencies and institutions with enough money to afford to purchase one.

The takeway here is that the first operating system to hit the market back in the 50s was GMOS. It was
