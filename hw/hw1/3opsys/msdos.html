MS-DOS

Main The First Operating System MS-DOS Windows
When PCs began to hit the market, IBM needed an operating system
for its line of personal computers. Microsoft bought the rights to
86-DOS from Seattle Computer Products, and began to tailor it to IBM's
requests. Under license, IBM released it as PC DOS 1.0.

MS-DOS became the most widely used of the DOS family of operating systems.
These OSs relied on floppy disks to store and access the operating system.
Even as it began to fade out of the market, it remained as the underlying
OS that the first versions of Windows would be based on.
